About Me

My name is Jeremy Wagerik and I am a Data Analysis and Data Visualizations Specialist.

I started working with data as a Loss Prevention Manager. I would run queries in our database progams, then manipulate and create tables or charts in Excel and VBA. As my skills advanced, I wanted to expand my knowledge and learn how to create the dashboards myself instead of having to ask someone else to make changes to them. This motivated me to go back to school and get my Certificate in Data Science and Visualizations from Rutgers University.

I am passionate about creating dashboards that are easy to understand and display meaningful data. My programming language of choice is Python as I believe it is very versatile. I like using SQL Databases as well as Tableau for my visualizations. ALthough, I really love working with HTML and JavaScript to deploy GeoMaps and dashboards to display data.

BikeSharing Proposal

This project was creating visualizations for a proposal to open a Bike Sharing company in Des Moines. To complete this I used data from CitiBike in NYC and Tableau to show the final results. The data was broken down and displayed in various charts to show who uses the bikes, how often and the duration of rentals. This information was put together in a clear story backed up by the data so an informed decision came be made on how to make this new business successful.

Loan Predictions

This project will be focused on identifying/predicting whether a participant might default on their loan. The goal of using this topic is to create a Supervised Machine Learning model trained on the loan status of the applicant data. Then that model can be used to predict whether a future applicant should be approved based on their specific input features. We wanted to explore what factors are most important when deciding to grant a loan and if there are any similar characteristics of applicants who default.
